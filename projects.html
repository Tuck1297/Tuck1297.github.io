Computer Graphics - Lighting, Shaders, and Texture Mapping

Focused on working with WebGL, this assingment had an overall objective to implement the necessary code to accurately simulate real world lighting and textures on 3D objects. It is not easily seen on the images here but I focused the majority of my time in implementing the simulation of multiple lights for this project. My partner focused on mapping the textures such as the one that you see on the red cube.

In addition, my partner and I were tasked in adding our own three dimensional shapes. While my partner did the rhombus I worked on the yellow star seen on the right of both images. There were two main light settings that we implemented. The first is called gouraud shading while the other is called phong shading and they are essentially two of the most common kinds of lighting calculations that one would find being used for online applications.

World Cinema Final Essay

Throughout the semester in a course called World Cinema I was given the opportunity to watch films made throughout the last few decades in many different countries including France, Japan, and Hong Kong. As a final project for the class, I was allowed to choose a country in which I would watch two films and compare them. I choose the country China because of their emphasis on martial arts films. One was titled Hero and the other was titled Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon. Throughout the essay I compared how beyond the martial arts these films emphasized different cultural aspects that would have been seen in the time eras in which these films were placed (between 1046 BCE to 206 BCE). Overall, my favorite part of this assignment was watching the films and seeing another culture's films that I previously never knew about.

Interaction with Graphics - Creative Coding Final Assignment

Interaction with Graphics is a multimedia installation that immerses the audience in the virtual realm of computer graphics through auditory, visual, and interactive queues. This time sequenced project, which is entirely written in Javascript with a library called ThreeJS, implements a variety of abstract and structured sequences, all of which demonstrate mathematical and programming achievements made in the last 30 years, such as perlin noise and sophisticated 3-dimensional model rendering. The main objective of this endeavor is to remind its audience of the past while demonstrating what the future holds.

The interactive queues implemented in this assignment include musical graphic syncronization by using Web Audio API and hand interaction through the library called HandsFree.
